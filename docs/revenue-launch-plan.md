# ReadNest Revenue Launch Plan

ReadNest is ready to move from product polishing into a small, measurable launch. The goal is not broad advertising first. The goal is to prove that parents and teachers understand the value, try the free activities, create accounts, and upgrade when personalization or teacher insight matters.

## Current Readiness

Verified on July 7, 2026:

- Production site is live at `https://myreadnest.org/`.
- Dev site is live at `https://wayne9999.github.io/early_Reader/`.
- Static SEO pages, sitemap, canonical tags, and JSON-LD validation pass.
- Search submission workflow runs successfully for IndexNow and Google Search Console.
- Donation checkout opens a Stripe Checkout page.
- Family Plus and Teacher Pro require sign-in before checkout, so subscriptions can be attached to the correct Firebase user.
- Guest users can use the public starter activities.
- Premium routes are protected from guest access.
- Local quality checks passed: `npm run check`, `npm run test`, `npm run test:e2e`, and `npm run build`.

## Positioning

Primary promise:

> Help young readers build confidence in 5-10 playful minutes a day, while parents and teachers see what needs practice next.

Parent angle:

- Short sessions that do not feel like homework.
- A clear path for kindergarten through grade 2.
- Progress summaries that tell parents what to practice next.
- Free starter activities before payment.

Teacher/tutor angle:

- Assigned-student progress visibility.
- Strengths, growth areas, and report support.
- Human-in-the-loop insight, with AI support only as a future recommendation aid.
- Teacher Pro is for people managing multiple learners, not casual browsing.

## Offers

- Free: public Reading and Memory, plus registered starter activities where allowed.
- Family Plus: premium activities, personalized paths, progress tracking, printable plans, and future premium packs.
- Teacher Pro: classroom dashboard, assigned-student analysis, reports, intervention planning, and future AI summaries.
- Donations: mission support for free starter access, hosting, accessibility, and classroom tools.

## First 30 Days

Week 1: Trust and conversion

- Manually test signup, Google/Facebook auth, student role setup, teacher role setup, donation, and subscription checkout in production.
- Add two short demo videos: parent view and child activity view.
- Add three real screenshots to the homepage and SEO pages.
- Ask 5-10 parents or teachers to try the free activities and send blunt feedback.
- Add at least three testimonial placeholders only after real feedback exists.

Week 2: Organic distribution

- Post in local parent groups, homeschool groups, tutoring communities, and teacher communities where self-promotion is allowed.
- Share one direct free-activity link: `https://myreadnest.org/#/reading`.
- Share one trust/value link: `https://myreadnest.org/online-reading-games/`.
- Ask users to try one activity with a child and report where the child smiled, got stuck, or lost attention.

Week 3: Content engine

- Publish three helpful articles or static pages:
  - "5-minute sight word practice for kindergarten"
  - "How to help a first grader sound out CVC words"
  - "Reading games that do not feel like homework"
- Add internal links from each article to the free activity and Family Plus page.
- Review Search Console queries for impressions and use them as article ideas.

Week 4: Small paid test

- Run a small Meta campaign to parents of K-2 children, capped at a strict daily budget.
- Use one conversion goal: account signup or checkout click, not vanity traffic.
- Test two messages:
  - Confidence: "Five happy minutes to help your child feel like a reader."
  - Clarity: "Know what your child should practice next."
- Stop any campaign that does not produce signups or meaningful trials.

## Channel Plan

Organic search:

- Keep expanding high-intent pages around grade, skill, and parent problem queries.
- Use Google Search Console to find queries with impressions but low clicks.
- Improve page titles and intros before writing too much new content.

Local/community:

- Parent Facebook groups, homeschool groups, school community pages, tutoring centers, churches/community centers, libraries, and after-school programs.
- Offer a simple "try this free reading game tonight" message instead of a hard sales pitch.

Teacher/tutor partnerships:

- Reach out to tutors and early elementary teachers who can test Teacher Pro with a small student group.
- Offer a founder discount or free month in exchange for structured feedback.
- Ask what report wording parents actually understand.

Paid acquisition:

- Start with Meta because parent targeting and local/community creative fit the product.
- Use TikTok later only if short activity-demo clips perform organically.
- Keep paid tests small until the funnel is proven.

Email:

- Capture parent emails only after signup or support contact.
- Send weekly progress reminders and one suggested 5-minute practice.
- Do not include child-sensitive details in marketing email subject lines.

## Metrics

Track weekly:

- Homepage visits.
- Free activity starts.
- Free activity completions.
- Account signups by role.
- Teacher profile completions.
- Paywall views.
- Checkout clicks.
- Successful subscription activations.
- Donation clicks and completed donations.
- Support cases by category.
- Search Console impressions, clicks, and indexed pages.

Target early benchmarks:

- 20-30 percent of homepage visitors start a free activity.
- 5-10 percent of free activity users create an account.
- 2-5 percent of account creators start checkout.
- At least 5 qualitative feedback responses before scaling ads.
